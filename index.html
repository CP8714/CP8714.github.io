The Lichtenburg Woodburning Machine


In recent years, DIY fractal wood burning has become popularized in various forms of online media, contributing to a rise in fatalities due to electrocution from non-professionals attempting the process. Our design project proposes the design and construction of a Lichtenberg machine (the device used for fractal wood burning) to reduce and control the risks associated with this practice. The primary objective of this project is to explore the pitfalls and weaknesses with typically DIY Lichtenberg machines, and develop an alternative that doesn’t compromise on the desired fractal burn patterns, while considerably improving safety through hardware and software protections, redundancies, and failsafes. In addition to the prioritized focus on safety and operation of the Lichtenberg machine, the project delves into the details of custom transformer design, the handling of testing of high voltage systems, risk analysis, DC to AC converter topologies, and power electronics. The project’s research and test results demonstrate decreasing muscle contraction and nerve stimulation as electrical frequency increases, high voltage insulation properties of various materials, proper operation of a purpose built transformer, and the operation of a resonant tank converter to increase output frequency.
